About

I’m a PhD candidate in the engineering department at the University of Cambridge. I’m interested in solving problems that maximise expected human prosperity. In pursuing solutions to such problems I’m method-agnostic. That said, Reinforcement Learning is proving useful, so my PhD focuses on improving the sample efficiency of RL algorithms. Current areas of interest include:

  • Zero-shot RL: solving tasks with no online training
  • Foundation models in RL: using arbitrary-length input sequences to do multi-modal RL

If you’re into this sort of thing, I’m always keen to chat so send me an email (srj38 (at) cam(dot)ac(dot)uk).
