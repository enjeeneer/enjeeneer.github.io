About

I’m a PhD candidate at the University of Cambridge interested in Reinforcement Learning (RL) and its real-world applications. I’m part of Jonathan Cullen’s group–the Resource Efficiency Collective –who look at scalable climate change mitigation solutions. I’m affiliated with the Alan Turing Institute, Cambridge’s AI for Environmental Risks centre, I’ve collaborated with the Cambridge Centre for the Study of Existential Risk (CSER), and work closely with the Control and Verification Group in Oxford’s Department of Computer Science.

My recent work has looked at RL for energy-system control, specifically controlling buildings such that they interact with the grid optimally, drawing power from the grid when grid carbon intensity is low. This setting, as with most real-world environments, is non-stationary, non-episodic and has strict safety requirements, making for really interesting theoretical challenges.

If you’re into this sort of thing, I’m always keen to chat so send me an email.

Scott
